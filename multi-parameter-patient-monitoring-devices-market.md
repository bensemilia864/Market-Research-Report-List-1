<p><p>Multi-Parameter Patient Monitoring Devices are categorized into high-acuity, mid-acuity, and low-acuity monitors based on their complexity and intended use. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients, offering advanced features like real-time data analysis and invasive measurements. Mid-acuity monitors serve less critical patients, providing essential parameter tracking without extensive features. Low-acuity monitors are used in general care settings for patients with stable conditions, focusing on basic vital sign monitoring. Each type serves distinct healthcare needs to enhance patient care.</p></p>
