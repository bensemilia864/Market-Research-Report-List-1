<p><p>The polyurethane spray equipment market is primarily divided into high pressure and low pressure spray equipment segments. High pressure spray equipment operates at elevated pressures, ensuring a finer atomization of polyurethane, which results in smoother finishes and improved adhesion. This type is ideal for large-scale applications. In contrast, low pressure spray equipment operates at reduced pressures, offering better control and reducing overspray, making it suitable for smaller projects or intricate applications where precision is essential.</p></p>
