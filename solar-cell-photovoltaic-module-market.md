<p><p>The solar cell (photovoltaic) module market encompasses various technologies tailored for energy conversion. Mono-Si modules, made from a single silicon crystal, offer high efficiency and space-saving benefits. Multi-Si modules, composed of multiple silicon crystals, are more cost-effective but slightly less efficient. CdTe modules utilize cadmium telluride, providing a low-cost alternative. CIGS modules, based on copper indium gallium selenide, are flexible and lightweight. Meanwhile, a-Si modules are made from amorphous silicon, offering versatility and lower production costs. Other technologies may include emerging materials and innovations.</p></p>
