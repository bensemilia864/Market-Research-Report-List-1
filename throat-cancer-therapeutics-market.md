<p><p>The throat cancer therapeutics market encompasses three primary treatment types: surgery, radiotherapy, and chemotherapy. Surgery involves the physical removal of tumors and affected tissues to achieve local control of cancer. Radiotherapy utilizes high-energy radiation to kill cancer cells and shrink tumors, often used post-surgery or as a primary treatment. Chemotherapy employs drugs to target and destroy rapidly dividing cancer cells, which may be administered before or after surgery or in combination with radiotherapy to enhance treatment efficacy and manage symptoms.</p></p>
