<p><p>The train disc brake linings market comprises three primary types: powder metallurgy, cast iron, and composite materials. Powder metallurgy offers high wear resistance and heat dissipation, making it suitable for high-performance applications. Cast iron is known for its cost-effectiveness and excellent braking performance, commonly used in conventional systems. Composite linings blend organic and inorganic materials, providing enhanced friction characteristics and reduced weight, catering to advanced rail systems. Each type caters to specific performance, cost, and weight requirements in railway applications.</p></p>
