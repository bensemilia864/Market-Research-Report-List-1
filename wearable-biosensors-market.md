<p><p>Wearable biosensors are categorized into single-use and repeated-use markets. Single-use biosensors are designed for one-time application, often providing immediate results, such as glucose monitoring patches or respiratory sensors. They are typically more convenient but may generate more waste. In contrast, repeated-use biosensors are durable devices that can be reused multiple times, like smartwatches or fitness trackers equipped with biomonitoring capabilities. These offer ongoing data collection and long-term tracking, making them favored for continuous health monitoring.</p></p>
